  1. John Stockton
    • John Stockton, widely regarded as one of the greatest point guards in NBA history, holds the league record for career assists and steals, showcasing his unparalleled court vision and defensive prowess.
    • Franchise Ranks: 1st in assists, 1st in steals, 2nd in points, 3rd in 3-pointers made, 5th in rebounds
  2. Karl Malone
    • Karl Malone, known as "The Mailman," is a basketball legend who dominated the NBA with his unparalleled scoring ability and physical presence, cementing his legacy as one of the greatest power forwards of all time.
    • Franchise Ranks: 1st in rebounds, 1st in points, 2nd in assists, 2nd in steals, 5th in blocks, 7th in field goal percentage
  3. Pete Maravich
    • Pete Maravich, often referred to as "Pistol Pete," captivated basketball fans with his dazzling ball-handling skills and scoring prowess, leaving an indelible mark on the game as one of its most iconic and innovative players.
    • Franchise Ranks: 8th in assists, 7th in points, 1st in triple-doubles
  4. Adrian Dantley
    • Adrian Dantley, a tenacious scorer known for his crafty post moves and adeptness at drawing fouls, left an enduring legacy in the NBA as one of the most efficient and consistent offensive forces of his era.
    • Franchise Ranks: 10th in assists, 3rd in points, 2nd in field goal percentage
  5. Rudy Gobert
    • Rudy Gobert, nicknamed the "Stifle Tower," has redefined the role of t he modern NBA center with his exceptional shot-blocking ability, defensive presence, and impact in the paint, earning multiple Defensive Player of the Year awards and becoming a cornerstone for the Utah Jazz franchise.
    • Franchise Ranks: 2nd in rebounds, 3rd in blocks, 10th in points, 1st in field goal percentage
